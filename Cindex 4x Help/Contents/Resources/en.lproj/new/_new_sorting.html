New features: sorting entries
 

Alphabetize text as other text
A major new Sort… option enables specified words or prefixes to words (e.g., “St.” or “Mc”) to be alphabetized as if they were other words or prefixes (e.g., “Saint” or “Mac”), without your having to provide text in {…} or <…> to override normal alphabetizing. You can also use this new capability to specify words that will be ignored altogether in alphabetizing.

Sorting complex page references
When sorting locators that included text to be ignored (i.e., text in <…>), Cindex formerly ignored that text entirely. It will now evaluate the text in <…> if it is the only thing that distinguishes two locators.

Ordering page references in bi-directional text
When index entries are in a left-to-right reading language (e.g., English) but contain passages of text in a right-to-left reading language (e.g., Arabic), page references may, depending on the placement of the right-to-left text, be ordered by the rules of the right-to left language. A new sorting option allows you to force left-to-right ordering of locators, regardless of language.
