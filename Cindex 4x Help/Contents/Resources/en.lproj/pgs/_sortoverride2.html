Case-sensitive alphabetizing
 

Cindex normally treats all letters as lowercase for the purposes of sorting records, though it attends to case if entries are otherwise identical. If it is important to distinguish upper- and lowercase versions of a letter (for example you wish all entries beginning with ‘A’ to come before all entries beginning with ‘a’), place a ~ immediately before the uppercase one to inhibit the implicit case conversion.

When Cindex distinguishes upper- and lowercase letters, the uppercase ones take precedence in the order AaBbCc....Zz.
