Checking punctuation
 

Checks on punctuation are available as part of a comprehensive check via Check Index… (Tools menu).

Open the panel, click the Basic tab, and configure the checks you want:

  • Misused special character(s). This checks that special Cindex characters ~ \ { } < > are correctly used in records (e.g., that a ~ isn’t the last character in a record field). This check cannot be disabled.
  • Misplaced or questionable punctuation. Finds such things as spaces before closing punctuation, repeated punctuation, and questionable sequences of punctuation characters.
  • Missing space before parentheses or brackets. Finds missing spaces. Cindex will ignore cases such as ‘Innovation(s)’ or ‘waltz(es)’, and will ignore any parenthetical suffixes to numbers, such as 401(k).
  • Mixed case words. Finds mixed-case words. This will not flag a word that has an initial cap only, or words that are plural acronyms (e.g., EFTs).

If Cindex finds errors it displays them in the check results. Each entry in the list identifies the source record and the nature of the error.

Click any item in the list to bring its record into view in the main document window; double-click the item (or hit RETURN) to open a window in which to edit its record.
