Formatting page ranges
 

Use the Page References panel (Document menu) to specify how ranges of page references should be formatted.

When you enter references in records you must use a single character (typically a hyphen, or minus sign) to denote a connected range of pages such as 55–67. When Cindex displays formatted entries, it translates the connecting character to another character or sequence of characters you specify. References that appear in records as 55-67 can therefore be displayed as 55–67, or 55 to 67, or 55 through 57.

Cindex can conflate references to consecutive pages, so that a series of references entered in records as

34, 33, 35, 37, 39, 40-42, 41-43

can appear as

33-35, 37, 39-43

Cindex can abbreviate the upper part of an inclusive range of page numbers, so that a range entered in a record as 920-923 might be displayed as 920-23.

Settings in the Ranges box control the formatting of inclusive ranges of page references.

Connecting text

Set Connecting Text to the character or sequence of characters that should represent a range in the formatted entry. When Cindex is first installed it is set to translate the hyphen to a single en-dash.

Conflate Beyond

Choose from the pop-up menu number of references to consecutive pages after which Cindex will start conflating them.

Abbreviation Rule

Choose the abbreviation rule from the pop-up menu. Abbreviation methods in the list are ordered least to most aggressive. Choose Chicago to use rules recommended by The Chicago Manual of Style or Hart to use the method recommended by Hart's Rules. Choose Full to abbreviate the second number to the smallest possible number of digits.
